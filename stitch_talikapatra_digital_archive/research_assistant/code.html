QUERY NO. 842 — 14:22

"I am investigating the administrative records of the Sringeri Sharada Peetham from the mid-14th century. Are there any specific mentions of land grants during the reign of Bukka Raya I?"

INTERNAL ARCHIVE SEARCH: COMPLETED REF: SR-1356-GRANTS

Based on the digital folios of the Sringeri collection, records indicate that Bukka Raya I issued several significant copper-plate inscriptions (Tamra-shasana) confirming land endowments to the Peetham between 1356 and 1370 [1].

One specific entry in the Kadata (cloth ledgers) describes the 'Vidyanagara Foundation Grant' which provided thirty villages for the sustenance of the Vedantic scholars [2]. These grants were often marked with the Varaha seal of the Vijayanagara Empire to ensure perpetual validity [3].
