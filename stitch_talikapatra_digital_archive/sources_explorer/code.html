The Periplus of the Erythraean Sea

Codex Fragmentum • AD 40—70

Of the designated ports on the Erythraean Sea, and the market-towns around the same, the first is the Egyptian port of Mussel Harbor. To those sailing from this place, the voyage to Berenice is toward the south, about 4,000 stadia. Both harbors are at the extremity of Egypt, and are bays of the Erythraean Sea.

Beyond Berenice, the coast curves in a great circle toward the east. The country of the Berbers, which is very long, is called Azania. Here there are no harbors, but only anchorages and watering-places. The natives are of great stature, and each has his own place of residence, and is governed by his own chief.

"The coast of the country of the Berbers is subject to the King of the Mapharitic region, who resides at the city of Muza." — Marginalia by Casson (1989)

From this point, sailing across the adjacent bay, there is another market-town of the Berbers, which is called Malao, distant a two days' sail; the anchorage is an open roadstead, sheltered by a spit running out from the east. Here the natives are more peaceable. There are imported into this place the things already mentioned, and also many tunics, and cloaks from Egypt, finished and dyed; drinking-cups, sheets of soft copper in thin layers, iron, and gold and silver coin, not much.

1 Erythraean Sea: The classical name for the Indian Ocean, including the Red Sea and Persian Gulf.

2 Stadia: A Greek unit of length, approximately 185 meters.

3 Muza: A major port city on the Red Sea in ancient Yemen.
