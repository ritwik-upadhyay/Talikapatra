The Founding of the Imperial Library

The initial decree for the centralization of provincial records was signed under the seal of the reigning monarch. This event marked the transition from oral tradition to a formalized archival system that would span centuries of bureaucratic evolution.

RECORD ID: MS-0911-C

The Great Scriptorium Fire

A catastrophic blaze in the southern wing resulted in the loss of 12% of the pre-imperial chronicles. This necessitated the first mass restoration project, leading to the creation of the illuminated copies we study today.

RECORD ID: MS-1044-B

Consolidation of the Guilds

Scholarly guilds from across the continent convened to standardize archival terminology. The 'Blue Ink Protocol' was established here, requiring all official transcriptions to use fermented indigo pigments for longevity.
